New passenger terminal + channel dredging for large cruise vessels. Targeted completion 2027.

East coast complex: hotels, marina, venues, F1 circuit, casino. Development starts 2026; first phase 2029.

What this means for buyers: infrastructure usually arrives before full residential completion. Buying pre-2028 in impacted zones (Chalong, Bang Tao, Thalang corridor) tends to capture uplift, but budget for construction noise and access changes.

Buyer's checklist

Before you sign anything.

Eight checks that separate a good deal from a costly mistake.

1. Title deed verified by a Thai lawyer
Chanote > Sor Kor. Confirm no liens, squatters, or company-share complications.
2. EIA approval is public record
If there’s no approved EIA, construction can’t legally start. Ask for the official reference number.
3. Developer track record, not just renderings
Visit a finished project. Ask for completion history, defect resolution, and current litigation.
4. Payment milestone protection
Never pay more than 30% before concrete foundation. Stage payments to construction progress.
5. Rental program reality-check
Get 12 months of actual STR income from the operator, not a projection. Ask about seasonality.
6. Fee structure transparency
Management fee, sinking fund, transfer fees, and taxes should be itemised before closing.
7. Resale liquidity in the sub-zone
Not all locations have buyer depth. Check 12-month resale activity and average days-on-market.
8. Insurance and warranty scope
Structural warranty, contents coverage, and title insurance are non-negotiable for financed or foreign buyers.

These checks are an overview, not legal advice. Always verify with a licensed Thai attorney.
